Japanese Fried Chicken

back to main

Total Time: 55 mins

Description

Crispy? Check. Sweet and Salty? Check. Delicious? CHECK!

Drenched in a garlicky soy glaze, this Japanese fried chicken is sure to leave you reaching for more - truly finger lickin' good!

Ingredients

  • 1 large egg (lightly beaten)
  • 1 cup all purpose flour (for coating)
  • 3 pounds chicken wings
  • 1 cup (16 tbsp) butter

Sauce

  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1/2 cup white vinecar
  • 3 tbsp soy sause
  • 3 tbsp water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder (or to taste)

Directions

  1. Bake bake bake!

    Preheat the oven 350F.

  2. Beat beat beat!

    Beat the egg in a small boul. Place flour in a shallow bowl.

  3. Dip dip dip!

    Dip each wing into the egg, then press it into the flour.

  4. BUTTER UP!

    Melt the butter in a large pan. Brown both sides of the chicken wings and place on a baking sheet.

  5. Sauce sauce sauce!

    Mix together sugar, vinegar, soy sauce, walter, salt, and garlic powder in a medium bowl. Pour over wings.

  6. Bake bake bake! (ROUND 2)

    Place wings in the preheated oven for 30 to 45 minutes (165F internal). Baste the wings frequently with the sauce.

  7. Eat eat eat!

    Now FEAST on your glorious creation! LONG LIVE THE EMPIRE!
